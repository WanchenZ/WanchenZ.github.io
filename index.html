About Me

I am a Mathematics PhD candidate graduating in May 2026. My work bridges the gap between Topology and practical Machine Learning applications. I focus on designing evaluation metrics grounded on structural properties of data and Wasserstein robustness of ML representations.

Research & Preprints

Wasserstein stability of persistence landscapes and barcodes

ArXiv: 2509.20921

  • Developed a new metric and stability framework for topological and vector-based data summaries.
  • Proved a 1-Lipschitz stability result: summaries are bounded by \(\frac{1}{2} W_p\) (Wasserstein distance) of the inputs.
  • Guarantees feature robustness for TDA applications in noisy ML environments.
Continuous persistence landscapes

ArXiv: 2512.00206

  • Generalized classical persistence landscapes to the continuous setting (asymptotic limit as $n \to \infty$).
  • Enhanced stability for large-scale TDA by modeling the limit behavior of data distributions.
  • Created a flexible framework for integrating topological features into high-volume ML pipelines.

Machine Learning Projects

Structural Analysis of Nonlinear Dynamical Couplings

Used persistent homology to identify couplings of multivariate time series data. Integrated topological features in the embedded space to classify the dynamical systems.

Geometric Wasserstein Autoencoder

Implementing a Variational Autoencoder (VAE) that utilizes the Wasserstein Distance (approximated by Sinkhorn Divergence) to learn latent representations of geometric manifolds, outperforming standard MSE loss in capturing point cloud topology.
